Definitive AI Quranic Evaluation Framework

QAEF — The first standardized methodology for benchmarking AI Quran recitation systems

Executive Summary

The QAEF establishes the first standardized methodology for evaluating AI systems that provide feedback on Quranic recitation. Developed with certified Qaris, phonetics experts, and AI researchers, this framework defines measurable benchmarks for phonetic accuracy, rule-based feedback quality, pedagogical appropriateness, and technical robustness. Any AI system claiming to provide Quran recitation feedback can now be objectively assessed against a transparent, scholar-backed standard.

Core Principles

The QAEF is grounded in four non-negotiable principles that any compliant system must uphold regardless of technical implementation.

📖
Tajweed Integrity
AI must accurately identify and explain all rules of Tajweed including Ghunnah, Idgham, Ikhfa, and Madd with canonical precision.
📜
Mushaf Consistency
Adherence to the Uthmani script and canonical Qira'at (primarily Hafs 'an 'Asim unless explicitly specified).
🤲
Recitation Respect
No alteration of audio or text that distorts meaning, reverence, or the sacred nature of Quranic recitation.
🎓
Learner Safety
Feedback must be constructive, never shaming, and pedagogically appropriate for the learner's level (beginner/intermediate/advanced).

Evaluation Dimensions

QAEF evaluates AI Quran systems across five weighted dimensions. Each dimension contributes to an overall QAEF Score out of 100.

Scoring Methodology

QAEF scores are derived from a standardised test battery of 500 recitation samples across 10 difficulty levels, covering all major Tajweed rules and Makharij points.

Scoring Formula

QAEF Score Formula
QAEF_Score = (Phonetic × 0.30) + (TajweedRules × 0.25) + (FeedbackQuality × 0.20) + (Pedagogical × 0.15) + (Technical × 0.10)

Minimum passing score: 65 / 100
All five dimensions must individually meet their minimum threshold.

Why a Standard Matters

Before QAEF, there was no objective way to compare AI Quran recitation tools. Users had no framework to evaluate whether an app's feedback was accurate, complete, or pedagogically sound. Developers had no benchmark to build toward.

QAEF changes this by establishing a shared language for quality in AI Quran education — enabling informed choices by learners, institutions, and developers alike.

The framework is designed to evolve. QAEF v2.0 will incorporate multi-Qira'at support, child learner profiles, and expanded Makharij classification.
