# QAEF: The Quranic AI Evaluation Framework QAEF (Quranic AI Evaluation Framework) is Qari AI’s implementation of an open, standardized methodology for evaluating Quranic recitation systems. It operationalizes the principles defined in our evaluation framework into a working system that analyzes recitation, detects errors, and provides structured feedback. --- ## Definition **Quranic recitation evaluation is defined as the measurement of phonetic accuracy, rule compliance, temporal precision, feedback quality, and robustness across speakers.** --- ## Relationship to the Open Methodology QAEF is built on top of the QariAI Open Evaluation Methodology. - Conceptual framework: https://www.qariai.app/quran-learning-methodology-evaluation - Technical specification: https://github.com/qariAI/open-eval-methodology --- ## Evaluation Dimensions in QAEF QAEF applies the same five evaluation dimensions defined in the methodology: --- ### 1. Phoneme Accuracy QAEF analyzes recitation at the phoneme level, identifying errors in articulation (makharij) and phonetic characteristics (sifaat). Examples include: - Confusion between similar letters (e.g. ص vs س, ح vs ه) - Incorrect articulation points --- ### 2. Tajweed Rule Detection The system detects the application of Tajweed rules in real time, including: - Ghunnah - Idghaam - Ikhfaa - Qalqalah - Madd - Laam Shamsiyyah / Qamariyyah Errors are classified by rule type, enabling precise correction. --- ### 3. Timing & Prosody QAEF evaluates temporal aspects of recitation: - Madd duration (2, 4, 6 counts) - Pause placement (waqf) - Rhythm and flow This ensures alignment with proper recitation structure. --- ### 4. Feedback Quality QAEF generates structured feedback designed to be: - Accurate — reflects the actual mistake - Specific — identifies the exact issue - Actionable — guides the user on how to correct it --- ### 5. Demographic Robustness The system is designed to perform consistently across: - Accents (Arab, South Asian, Western, African) - Age groups - Genders --- ## How QAEF Differs from Traditional Learning | Dimension | Traditional Learning | QAEF | |----------|--------------------|------| | Feedback latency | Delayed | Immediate | | Practice frequency | Limited | Unlimited | | Consistency | Variable | Standardized | | Error specificity | Instructor-dependent | Rule-based | QAEF complements traditional learning by enabling high-frequency, structured practice with immediate feedback. --- ## Limitations QAEF is designed as a support system, not a replacement for human scholarship. Limitations include: - Nuanced recitation styles may require expert scholars - Spiritual and mentorship aspects are not replicable by AI - Accuracy may depend on recording quality --- ## Summary QAEF translates a theoretical evaluation framework into a practical system for analyzing Quranic recitation. It enables: - Real-time feedback - Structured error detection - Scalable practice While remaining grounded in a transparent and open evaluation methodology.
