Quranic AI Evaluation Framework

QAEF is QariAI's implementation of an open, standardized methodology for evaluating AI Quranic recitation systems — the first of its kind.

Technical specification on GitHub →

Definition: Quranic recitation evaluation is the measurement of phonetic accuracy, rule compliance, temporal precision, feedback quality, and robustness across speakers.

Five Evaluation Dimensions
1

Phoneme Accuracy

QAEF analyzes recitation at the phoneme level, identifying errors in articulation points (makharij) and phonetic characteristics (sifaat).

ص vs س confusion ح vs ه confusion Incorrect makhraj
2

Tajweed Rule Detection

The system detects application of Tajweed rules in real time and classifies errors by rule type, enabling precise correction.

Ghunnah Idgham Ikhfa Qalqalah Madd Lam Shamsiyyah
3

Timing & Prosody

QAEF evaluates temporal aspects of recitation to ensure alignment with proper recitation structure.

Madd duration (2, 4, 6 counts) Waqf placement Rhythm & flow
4

Feedback Quality

QAEF generates structured feedback designed to be pedagogically actionable — not just flagging errors, but guiding correction.

Accurate Specific Actionable
5

Demographic Robustness

The system is designed to perform consistently regardless of speaker background.

Arab accents South Asian accents Western accents All age groups

QAEF complements traditional learning — it enables high-frequency structured practice, not a replacement for scholarship.

Limitations

QAEF is a support system, not a replacement for human scholarship.

  • Nuanced recitation styles and qira'at variations may require expert scholars
  • Spiritual and mentorship aspects of Tajweed are not replicable by AI
  • Accuracy depends on recording quality and ambient noise conditions

Built on the Open Evaluation Methodology

QAEF operationalizes the principles defined in QariAI's open methodology. Both documents are publicly licensed (CC BY 4.0) to enable transparent, reproducible benchmarking.
