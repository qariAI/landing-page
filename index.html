Common questions

Everything you need to know about AI-powered Quran recitation coaching.

How does AI tajweed work?

QariAI records your recitation and analyzes it against 24 tajweed rules — ghunnah, qalqalah, madd, shaddah, ikhfa, idgham, and others. Each rule is checked at the letter level, with severity scoring (Lahn Jali or Lahn Khafi) and coaching on mouth position. Unlike generic speech recognition, the analysis is tajweed-specific: it understands nasalization, bouncing sounds, elongation timing, and articulation points (makharij).

Can AI replace a Quran teacher?

No — AI complements a teacher, never replaces one. QariAI handles the repetitive mechanical feedback (was this letter correct? Is the madd exactly 2 counts?) so teachers can focus on what only humans can give: ijazah (chain of transmission), spiritual depth, encouragement, and advanced maqamat. See our teacher's guide to AI tajweed assessment.

What's the difference between Tajweed Mode and Hifz Mode?

Tajweed Mode scores pronunciation quality — how correctly you pronounced each letter. Hifz Mode tracks memorization accuracy — did you say the right words, in the right order? A forgotten word is not a pronunciation mistake, so they're scored separately. This is unique to QariAI; most apps conflate the two.

Is QariAI better than Tarteel?

They solve different problems. Tarteel is primarily a memorization companion. QariAI focuses on deep tajweed coaching with letter-level severity scoring and mouth-position guidance, plus a separate Hifz mode. Full comparison: QariAI vs Tarteel.

Does it work for non-Arabic speakers?

Yes. The interface is in English, Arabic, and French. Coaching explains mouth position, tongue placement, and airflow in plain language — you don't need to know Arabic grammar. Many users are non-native speakers learning from scratch.

How much does QariAI cost?

Free to start — 3 recitation sessions per day for guests, 5 per day when signed in. Pro unlocks unlimited sessions at £4.99/month or £29.99/year. Lifetime option also available. No ads, no data selling.

Which tajweed rules does it check?

All 24 standard tajweed rules: madd (natural, permissible, obligatory, necessary), ghunnah, qalqalah, ikhfa, iqlab, idgham (with and without ghunnah), idgham shafawi, idgham mutajanisayn, idgham mutaqaribayn, laam shamsiyah, hamzat wasl, and others. See the full rule list.

Is my audio data private?

Yes. Audio is sent to our analysis engine for scoring and deleted immediately after. We don't store your recitations, train on them, or sell any data. See the full privacy policy.
